Ema Datshi

Bhutan's iconic chili & cheese stew. Serves 2–3 • Easy • ~35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 8–10 green chili peppers (adjust to spice tolerance)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or butter
  • Salt to taste
  • 100–150 g cheese (traditionally yak). Substitutes: feta, processed cheese, or a mix of cheese and cream.

Tip: If yak cheese isn't available, many use Amul cheese, cheddar, or Laughing Cow to get the right creamy texture.

Steps

  1. Prep: Rinse chilies. For milder heat, slit and remove some seeds. Slice onion and garlic; chop tomato.
  2. Base: Heat oil/butter in a pan over medium. Sauté onion 2–3 min until translucent; add garlic 30–60 sec.
  3. Chilies: Add chilies and stir 1–2 min. Add tomato.
  4. Simmer: Pour in 1 cup water, season with salt, cover, and simmer 5–8 min until chilies soften.
  5. Cheese: Reduce heat low. Add cheese in pieces. Cover and gently melt 2–4 min without boiling.
  6. Finish: Stir until creamy. Adjust salt and consistency with a splash of water or cream if needed.
  7. Serve hot with red rice or buckwheat.
